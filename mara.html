Maasai Mara Holidays

Overview

Covering an area of 1,510 square kilometers Masai Mara National Reserve is located in the southwest of Kenya, 180 km from the capital city Nairobi with breathtaking sceneries and a vast species of both flora and Fauna (plants and animals).The name of the Reserve is derived from two words: Masaai which is the dominant community that lives in the Region near the park and with a Rich cultural history attached to the park and the word MARA which is a Masaai name meaning spotted. The park can be accessed through its six gates, which are, the Sekenani gate, Talek gate, Musiara gate, oloololo gate, olaimutia gate and Sand river gate.

Natural Attractions

The Masai Mara National Reserve is home to some of the world’s most breathtaking sights and a vast variety of wildlife and activities which make it a must go destination. The attractions that you will be able to experience and witness at the Mara include: This is the annual Movement of wildebeest in the Masai Mara – Serengeti ecosystem which involves the crossing of the famous Mara River by the wildebeest which makes up the largest population of the herd and zebras, elands, Thompson Gazelles, Grant’s gazelle and impalas. The crossing of the River is usually the climax of the whole Migration which occurs annually between December and March or May and November, all dependent on the rain patterns and the seasons which are unpredictable.
